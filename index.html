Geography of India - Wikipedia

India is a country of great physical diversity. The main land comprises four regions: the great mountain zone, plains of the Ganga and the Indus, the desert region, and the southern peninsula.

Physiographic Divisions: The Himalayas, Indo-Gangetic Plains, Thar Desert, and the Peninsular Plateau form the major structural divisions of India.

The Himalayan mountain range is about 2,400 km long and consists of three almost parallel ranges interspersed with large plateaus and valleys.
