Geography of India - Wikipedia

India is situated on the Indian Plate, the northern part of the Indo-Australian Plate, whose continental crust forms the Indian subcontinent.

Physical Regions: The main land comprises four regions, namely: the great mountain zone, plains of the Ganga and the Indus, the desert region and the southern peninsula. The Himalayan mountain range, about 2,400 km long, consists of three almost parallel ranges.

India's coast line is 7,517 kilometers (4,700 miles) long; 5,423 kilometers (3,400 miles) belong to peninsular India and 2,094 kilometers (1,300 miles) to the Andaman, Nicobar, and Lakshadweep island chains.
