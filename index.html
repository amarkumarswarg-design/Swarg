Geography of India - Physical Regions

India is a country of great physical diversity. The main land comprises four regions, namely: the great mountain zone, plains of the Ganga and the Indus, the desert region and the southern peninsula.

The Himalayan Mountain Range: It consists of three almost parallel ranges interspersed with large plateaus and valleys, some of which, like the Kashmir and Kullu valleys, are fertile, extensive, and of great scenic beauty.

The Indo-Gangetic Plains: These are formed by the basins of three major rivers—the Indus, the Ganga, and the Brahmaputra. They are among the world's greatest stretches of flat alluvium and also one of the most densely populated areas on earth.
