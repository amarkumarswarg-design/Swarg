Geography of India - Wikipedia

India is a country of great physical diversity. The main land comprises four regions, namely: the great mountain zone, plains of the Ganga and the Indus, the desert region and the southern peninsula.

Physiographic Divisions: The Himalayan mountain range, Indo-Gangetic plains, and the Peninsular Plateau form the major structural divisions of the country. India's geography is highly varied, with snow-capped mountain ranges, deserts, plains, and rainforests.

The Great Mountain Zone: It consists of three almost parallel ranges interspersed with large plateaus and valleys, some of which, like the Kashmir and Kullu valleys, are fertile and extensive.
